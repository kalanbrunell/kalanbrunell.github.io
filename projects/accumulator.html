High-Voltage EV Accumulator

Team: Tufts Electric Racing Competition: Formula-Hybrid Electric Role: Accumulator Project Manager (2025 — Present), prev. Electrical Engineer (2024 — 2025) In Progress

Designing a competition-grade EV accumulator that prioritizes safety, performance, and serviceability under FSAE regulations — from cell architecture and BMS integration to fusebox layout and high-voltage interlocks.

Project mission: engineer a competition-grade EV accumulator that prioritizes safety, performance, and serviceability while meeting strict FSAE regulatory requirements.

Current accumulator May 2025 — Present

As Accumulator Project Manager, I'm leading the design of a fully removable 440-cell, 450 V accumulator for our Formula SAE Electric racecar. The new pack is a clean-sheet redesign aimed at outperforming our off-the-shelf options on size, weight, and power efficiency — and at giving the team an accumulator that can be pulled from the car as a single serviceable unit.

Pack

Removable 440-cell, 450 V architecture built around Molicel P42A cells.

Custom BMS

STM32-based board handling cell monitoring, balancing, charge control, and CAN telemetry.

Thermal Network

Custom RS-485 protocol for real-time temperature mapping & fault reporting across 4 distributed thermistor boards.

vs. OTS

Notable improvements in size, weight, and power efficiency over off-the-shelf BMS / pack solutions.

The custom STM32 BMS replaces a chain of off-the-shelf modules with a single integrated board that handles cell monitoring, passive balancing, charger sequencing, and CAN telemetry to the rest of the vehicle.

Rather than wiring every thermistor back to a central controller, four distributed thermistor boards communicate over a custom RS-485 protocol I engineered for the pack — giving us continuous temperature mapping and fault reporting without flooding the main loom.

Cell testing & validation

Validating the Molicel P42A under realistic Formula-Hybrid load profiles is essential before the pack architecture is locked. Our test protocol evaluates discharge performance across temperature ranges, load conditions, and cycle counts — examining voltage sag and capacity retention.

The data feeds directly into pack architecture, cooling, and BMS configuration decisions, keeping the pack reliable through an event while staying compliant with FSAE safety regulations.

Previous accumulator 2024 — 2025
